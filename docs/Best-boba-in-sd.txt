Camellia Rd (Camellia)
Highlights: Loose leaf tea, gets the "tea" part right, amazing Thai milk tea, perfectly chewy and sweetened boba. They also feature monthly flavors.
Parking: Large, sufficient parking.

Wushiland (Oo Tea)
Highlights: Consistency champ, roasted oolong is "top tier" and favorite in all of SD. Friendly staff, app ordering, lots of customizations, not overly trendy/sweet.
Location: Inside the old 99 Ranch (not UTC).
Parking: Good parking.

3CAT Tea (3 Cat)
Highlights: Best overall quality, known for their mochi drinks. "Not traditional but completely rad."
Location: Convoy.
Parking: Atrocious.

Yun Tea House
Highlights: Rated 10/10 for experience and tea selection. Often has a long line.
Location: Convoy.
Parking: Tough parking. A private lot where they might try to ticket you ($80) if staying over 2 hours. Alternate parking on Armour street or alleyway to Hmart.

Omomo
Highlights: Very popular, trendy, and sweet drinks. Often has a long line.
Location: Carmel Valley / Del Mar Highlands.
Parking: Large, sufficient parking.

Daboba
Highlights: Roasted brown sugar series is highly recommended. Great alternative if you want to avoid long lines.
Locations: Mira Mesa, Kearny Mesa, Rancho Bernardo.

Bopomofo
Highlights: Unique flavors.
Location: Convoy.
Parking: "The biggest emptiest parking lot you will find on Convoy."

Bei Yuan Tea and Boba (formerly Tea Station)
Highlights: Fresh boba, quality tea that doesn't just taste like sugary drinks. Recommend going shortly after they open for lunch for maximum freshness.

Dayung & Moge
Highlights: Great for fruit teas.
Location: Dayung in Scripps/Mira Mesa.
Parking: Both have large sufficient parking.

Bengong's Tea
Highlights: Considered by one user as one of the best right now, alongside 3CAT. Good fruit tea.
Location: UTC.
Parking: Large, sufficient parking.

The Alley
Highlights: Ube milk tea is highly praised.

Me*Me Tea and Dessert
Highlights: Good drinks every time, big menu, always use real fruit in their tea blends.
Location: Genesee Ave.
Parking: Has a real parking lot.

Tea's Moment
Highlights: Rose-flavored milk tea with cheese foam.
Location: Point Loma.

Jungle Tea
Highlights: Locally owned/not a chain. Lots of variety and customization options.
Location: Mira Mesa.

General Criteria for "Good" Boba (According to the thread):
Boba Quality: Texture, chewiness, freshness, and right level of sweetness. (Homemade boba is a plus).
Tea & Ingredients: Quality of tea leaves, brewing technique, and using real ingredients (e.g., real fruit vs. syrups, real taro vs. powdered flavorings).
